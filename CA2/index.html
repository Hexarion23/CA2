The beginning: the 1950s
In the aftermath of World War II, as the world moved away from traditional, entrepot trade towards industrialization, the importance of technical education became apparent to many individuals within the government and the private sector. In 1951, businessmen met at the Adelphi Hotel in order to discuss the possibility of a polytechnic based in Singapore in order to train skilled, local workers.



The following year, Mr Thio Chan Bee petitioned the government for a polytechnic, a suggestion that was accepted. In 1954, the government passed the Singapore Ordinance, which led to the official establishment of SP, Singapore's first polytechnic. Four years later, in 1958, 2800 students registered for 58 courses and the campus was completed. A few months later in 1959, Prince Philip officially opened SP and Dr Toh Chin Chye took over chairmanship of Board of Governors.
Brave new world: The 1960s
The 60s represented a decade of growth and restructuring for SP. Being the first polytechnic, SP needed to be efficient and notable in order to maintain its necessity. The first Graduation Ceremony on 26 August 1961 was held at Victoria Theatre. In 1963, craft courses transferred to Singapore Vocational Institute to make way for expansion and upgrading of professional and technician courses and Mr A R Edis was appointed Principal.



Through 1964 to 1968, Dr C A Hart led Colombo Plan team to study development of advanced courses. University of Singapore looked into feasibility of a faculty of technology and granting university degrees to Polytechnic graduates. Professional diploma courses gradually replaced by degree courses and the first batch of degree graduates produced. A decision was taken to transfer degree courses to University of Singapore, giving rise to the Faculties of Architecture and Engineering. In 1968 and 1969, Singapore Polytechnic was restructured into School of Industrial Technology and School of Nautical Studies and Industrial and Business Orientation Programme was launched by Dr Toh Chin Chye, Minister for Science and Technology.
Expanding out: The 1970s
For SP, the 70s represented a time of great growth and development. In 1971, Nautical Studies, Civil Engineering and Building students were housed in Princess Mary Barracks and the campus site at Dover Road was chosen as permanent home of the Polytechnic. By the end of 1971, Princess Mary Barracks at Dover Road was converted into Princess Mary Campus - SP’s second campus. From 1972 to 1979, extensive expansion and construction continued. Ayer Rajah Road was campus completed and SP began functioning on three campuses.



Mr V P W Ager was appointed Principal and construction of Dover Road campus began. Foundation stone for new campus was laid by Dr Toh Chin Chye on 12 April 1975 and Mr Low Guan Onn was appointed Chairman of the Board of Governors. In 1978, the Dover Road campus was completed and Mr Khoo Kay Chai confirmed first local Principal after two years in acting capacity. Dover Road campus officially opened by Prime Minister Lee Kuan Yew on 7 July 1979. The ceremony was held at the Quadrangle between the Admin Building and the Library.
Maintaining a steady course: the 1980s
The 80s represented a period of steady growth and expansion, as well as some restructuring. In 1980, Singapore Polytechnic (Amendment) Act was passed in Parliament on 26 February, reconstituting the Board of Governors and reorganising student bodies. Mr Chua Chor Teck was appointed Chairman of the Board of Governors the same year. In 1982, Five-year Expansion Plan (1982-86) was approved, detailing new targets for student enrolment and staffing, as well as new courses. Two years later, all full-time Technician Diploma courses were restructured and revised to cater for high technology needs.



In 1986, Prof George Fong Wah took over as Chairman of the Board of Governors, the Second Phase campus development programme was completed which took five years and SP was visited by His Excellency, Mr Wee Kim Wee, then President of the Republic of Singapore. In 1987, Japan-Singapore Institute of Software Technology (JSIST) was transferred to Singapore Polytechnic. The following year, Graduates' Guild's clubhouse was officially opened by then Senior Minister of State for Education Dr Tay Eng Soon.
A decade of milestones: the 1990s
The 90s represented ten years of quick growth and the establishment of many organisations for post-graduation. It represented a decade of milestones. In 1990, the new $3.3 million Sports Complex was completed and the 50,000th graduate received his diploma at the 30th Graduation Ceremony. In 1991, the Dual Training Scheme for workers to study part-time at the Polytechnic and receive on-the-job training at their companies was introduced and SP became the first poly to offer a professional Diploma in Accountancy.



In 1992, then Deputy Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong officially opened the Third Phase campus development project and the Singapore Polytechnic Entrepreneurs' Club for graduates who had become successful entrepreneurs was established the next year. In 1994, SP became the first poly to introduce the Diploma in Optometry. In 1995, Principal Khoo Kay Chai retired after 19 years at the helm and Mr Low Wong Fook took over as the new Principal the following year. In 1999, in recognition of its attainment of a commendable level of performance in its journey to excellence, Singapore Polytechnic was made a member of Singapore Quality Class.
Achievements and accolades: the 2000s
The 2000s represented a decade of many awards and honours for SP and its graduates. In 2001, the Polytechnic satisfied requirements for ISO 9000 and ISO 14000 certifications and the Industrial Training Programme commemorated its 30th anniversary. Next year, the Public Service Award for Organisational Excellence, one of the most prestigious service wide awards, was presented to SP. In 2003, Principal lecturer, Mr Loh Peng Chum created the world’s first titanium-gold and five-coloured multi-layered gold. The following year, in 2004, 20 years ago, Singapore Polytechnic turned 50. His Excellency, Mr S R Nathan, then President of the Republic of Singapore, launched the yearlong celebrations on 13 February at the official opening of the new Convention Centre and Plaza.



In 2006, Singapore Polytechnic was awarded the Best Environmental Report for 2005 by the Association of Chartered Certified Accountants. In 2007, The SP Scholarship Programme was launched with the first batch of 40 SP Scholars receiving their scholarships. Mr Tan Hang Cheong took over as the new Principal at the start of 2008. In 2009, Chua Koon Ting became the first polytechnic student to read Dentistry at the National University of Singapore and Kuriakin made history by becoming the first student to score distinctions for all his subjects.
Restructuring and new appointments: the 2010s
The 2010s represented a decade of new opportunities and growth for SP. In 2011, Singapore Polytechnic received the President’s Social Service Award 2011 for the voluntary contributions made to the social service sector. The following year, a new Board of Board Governors was appointed. Outgoing chairman Mr Tan Kay Yong, who dedicated 12 years of distinguished service, was replaced by Mr Bill Chang. Six new members also came on board. In 2013, SP was awarded the Public Service Milestone Award at that year's Excellence in Public Service Awards.



In 2014, SP celebrated its 60th anniversary and SP’s new vision – A caring community of inspired learners committed to serve with mastery was announced. The following year, SP reached a key milestone with graduation of close to 5,900 students. In 2016, Mr Soh Wai Wah took over as the new Principal and CEO in October 2016. Two years later in 2018, SP achieved its 200,000th graduate milestone.
Present day: the 2020s
I can't really describe the 2020s like I did the other decade, owing to the fact that we're still halfway through. However, I can write about the milestones that we've had over the past few years. In 2020, Ms Janet Ang appointed as the new chairman on 16 September. 3 years later, SP launched the first decarbonisation playbook, paving the way for the Institute of Higher Learning. The following year, this year, SP celebrates its 70th anniversary.



In 2024, SP achieved the culmination of their efforts: my matriculation. HAHA just kidding. There isn't a lot to write about so I'm just gonna write down what SP means to me specifically. SP wasn't my dream school. In fact, I never wanted to go to a polytechnic in the first place. However, due to some circumstances, I had to. I chose SP, and enrolled into DIT soon after. To be honest, I was not expecting much of this school. Regardless, it blew me out of the water. Here, over the past few months, I've learned so much. I've grown as a person. I've found mentors and friends with whom I'm so excited to spend my next three years with. SP gave me another home. Thank you, SP.

Amanda Chia Siew Hui - Institutional Medallist, Diploma in Nutrition, Health and Wellness

Amanda delivering her valedictorian speech in 2015.

Amanda Chia founded the SP sign language club, through which today more than 100 members are learning to communicate with the physically handicapped using sign language. Ms Chia received the Temasek Education Foundation Sunburst Scholarship and was accepted to study in the NUS' School of Medicine. She was awarded the Singapore Food Manufacturers' Association Gold Medal and the Toh Chin Chye Gold Medal as well during her time in SP.
Chong Han Lyn - Institutional Medallist, Diploma in Media and Communication

Chong Han Lyn managing the communication panels in SP.

Chong Han Lyn worked on several projects during her time at SP, such as "Chance", a short film for the Temasek 20/20 Short Film Project, as well as a one-month social media campaign in partnership with Migrant Workers' Centre. She accepted Starcom's offer for the role of media executive. Ms Chong was awarded the Low Guan Onn Gold Medal and the Asia Fund Space Gold Medal during her time at SP.
Mohamed Najulah -Institutional Medallist, Diploma in Business Information Technology

Najulah and teammates being presented with the Microsoft YouthSparks Scholarship

Najulah participated in several competitions throughout his years at SP. He took part in the Data-Driven Innovation Challenge and the Transport & Youth Hackathon organised by the LTA. He, along with some classmates, developed an application called "Happy Wheel" that leveraged on publicly available data sets to suggest wheelchair-friendly paths to various locations. His efforts were rewarded with the People's Choice award and a commendation by Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong. Najulah decided to pursue a degree in Information Systems at SMU. He was awarded with the Chua Chor Teck Gold Medal and the IMDA Gold Medal during his time at SP.
Kwek Ai Ling -Institutional Medallist, Diploma in Experience and Product Design

Ai Ling being presented with her diploma and Institutional Medal at her graduation.

Kwek Ai Ling acted as Organising Chairman for the DXPD section of the SP Design School Graduation Showcase and participated in the Singaplural 48 Hours Challenge during the Singapore Design Week. She was awarded the NUS Global Merit Scholarship and went on to study Industrial Design at NUS. During Ai Ling's time at SP, she was awarded the Toh Chin Chye Gold Medal, the DBS Bank Gold Medal and the Norton Prize.
Pavan Singh Gill - Institutional Medallist, Diplomain Electrical and Electronic Engineering

Pavan Singh and Leong Guang Ho presenting a biochip they created at SP.

Pavan acted as Corporate Coordinator in SP Students' Union and became President of the Student Entrepreunership Club. He chaired a committee for the Youth Model ASEAN Conference, a regional gathering of youths from all 10 ASEAN states. He went on to pursue a degree in Electrical Engineering at NUS under the NUS Merit Scholarship. During his time at SP, he won the Lee Kuan Yew Award, the SMRT Gold Medal, the OCBC Prize and the Institution of Engineers Gold Medal Award.
Lin Tong Shen - Course Medallist, Diploma in Clean Energy

Lin Tong Shen at the Energy Market Authority.

Tong Shen created a solar cell from a silicon wafer and went on an industrial attachment trip to an energy company in China, as well as a second one at A*STAR. He pursued a degree of electrical and electronic engineering at NTU and the Energy Market Authority Undergraduate Scholarship. During his time at SP, Tong Shen won the A*STAR Science Award and the Singapore Power Prize.
Eileen Kong Ee Lin - Course Medallist, Diploma in Accountancy

Eileen at the SP70 DBA Homecoming Event.

Eileen completed her internship at KPMG LLP, one of the Big Four accounting firms in Singapore. She was also the President of the SP Business School Kindness Movement, and took part regularly in blood donation drives and entertaining senior citizens on trips to museums and the botanic gardens. She was offered places to study business administration in NTU and NUS. During her time in SP, she was awarded the ACCA Prize, the Far East Organisation Scholarship, the LEAD Scholarship and the Model Student Award.
